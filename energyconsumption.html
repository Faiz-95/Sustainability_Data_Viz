Consumption of Energy

In this visualisation, we analyse the total quantity of energy consumed in Australia from 1980 to 2019. Here, we provided a breakdown of the fuel types consumed over the years. We were able to visualise accurate data by using the Australian Energy Update 2021 Report from the Australian Government website as the data source for this infographic.

We have the time period in years on the x-axis and the amount of energy consumed in petajoules on the y-axis. By hovering over a fuel type in the legend, the area graph corresponding to that fuel type would have a different opacity, highlighting that fuel type while fading the others into the background. Additionally, brushing is added to the visualisation so that the viewer can zoom in on a particular time period. By double-clicking the graph, the default view will be restored.

Gigawatt hours, abbreviated as GWh, is a unit of energy representing one billion (1,000,000,000) watt hours and is equivalent to one million kilowatt hours. Gigawatt hours are often used as a measure of the output of large electricity power stations.
